The Concept

Fashion born from silence.

Mounam, meaning "silence" or "being silenced," is a project that explores restriction, control, and the struggle for self-expression through fashion. The name reflects the emotional starting point of the collection, inspired by witnessing people around me feel unheard, limited, or unable to fully express themselves because of societal expectations and restrictions.

Growing up in an open-minded environment while seeing friends experience these limitations created a strong contrast that shaped the direction of this project. Feelings of silence and powerlessness became translated into garments through the use of knots, ties, and rope-inspired constructions. These elements act as metaphors for restriction, representing something that can both hold together and hold back.

The project also connects these ideas to sustainability through zero waste pattern cutting. Designing within strict limitations became conceptually important, as the making process itself reflects the idea of restriction. Instead of seeing limitations as something negative, the project explores how they can lead to creativity, adaptability, and intention.

At the centre of Mounam is the idea of choice. Garments are designed to be multi-wear and interchangeable, allowing the wearer to style them in different ways, including transforming the same pattern into both a top and a skirt. This shift from restriction to freedom allows the wearer to reclaim control over how they present themselves.
